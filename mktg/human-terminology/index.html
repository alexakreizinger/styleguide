Guidelines about what makes us HUMAN.

Branding

Capitalize HUMAN

The name of our company is HUMAN or HUMAN Security.

Product names

Our product offerings include MediaGuard, FraudSensor, BotGuard for Growth Marketing, Botguard for Applications, and the HUMAN Mobile SDK.

Don’t abbreviate HUMAN’s product names in public-facing documentation.

  • No: BGforA protects your website or application from bots.

  • Yes: BotGuard for Applications protects your website or application from bots.

Other terminology

HUMAN doesn’t block

HUMAN’s products don’t directly block traffic; we merely analyze traffic and return our recommendation for how the user should respond. It’s up to our users to act on that recommendation.

As such, be careful not to imply that HUMAN’s products block traffic (or take any kind of action at all). Instead, emphasize that we provide a recommendation, often in the form of an actionable data point, which users can then choose whether to incorporate into their own workflows.

  • NO!!!: The Mitigation API will block all events that were classified as bots.

  • Yes: The Mitigation API will return a block response for all events that were classified as bots.

Bots aren’t always bad

Don’t use the word bot to mean bad bot; if you’re referring to a bad bot, use a descriptive adjective like bad or malicious. Similarly, if you’re referring to a good or neutral bot, use a descriptive adjective like benign or helpful.

Hackers aren’t always bad, either

Good hackers exist—many of them work for HUMAN! Don’t use the word hacker to mean bad hacker; if you’re referring to a bad hacker, use a descriptive adjective like dangerous or malicious. You can also use general terms like fraudster, cybercriminal, or bad actor.

Signals vs. data

The phrase collect signals is preferable to the phrase collect data (since the latter often has unfortunate implications).

Words to avoid

  • Don’t refer to hackers as white hat or black hat.
  • Don’t use words like blacklist or whitelist; instead, say deny list or allow list.
